Answers to the most common questions from Ghanaians living or planning to live in Spain.

To study in Spain, you first need to apply and get accepted by a Spanish educational institution. Once accepted, apply for a student visa (visado de estudios) at the Spanish consulate in Ghana. You'll need: acceptance letter, valid passport, proof of funds, health insurance, and the visa application form. After arriving in Spain, apply for your TIE (student residence card) within 30 days.
Start the renewal process 60 days before your TIE expires. Book an appointment at the Oficina de Extranjería via the Sede Electrónica. You'll need: current TIE, passport, NIE, proof of enrollment or continued studies, proof of funds, and completed EX-17 form. Pay the fee using Modelo 790. After submitting, you'll receive a receipt (resguardo) valid while you wait.
Submit your original degree, official Spanish translation by a sworn translator, Apostille/legalisation, and proof of identity to the Ministerio de Educación. The process can take several months. Check the Downloads section for the direct link to start.
Yes — students can work up to 30 hours per week without a separate work permit as long as it doesn't affect your studies. After your first year you can apply for an autorización de trabajo. Always register with Seguridad Social when starting any job.
Most banks require NIE/TIE, passport, and proof of address. Wise and N26 are popular with international students — no Spanish address needed, no monthly fees, and great exchange rates for sending money to Ghana.
Empadronamiento is registering your address at your local Ayuntamiento (town hall). It's required for healthcare, certain immigration processes, and proof of residence. Bring your passport/TIE and rental contract. It's completely free.
Go to your local Centro de Salud with your TIE, NIE, and empadronamiento certificate to register and receive your tarjeta sanitaria (health card). Many student visas also require private health insurance from the start.
African food stores exist in most major Spanish cities, usually near main train stations or in multicultural neighbourhoods. Join our WhatsApp group — community members in your city will know exactly where to go!
