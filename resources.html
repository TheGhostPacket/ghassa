Info

Answers to the most common questions from Ghanaians living or planning to live in Spain.

To study in Spain, you first need to apply and get accepted by a Spanish educational institution. Once accepted, you apply for a student visa (visado de estudios) at the Spanish consulate in Ghana. You'll need: acceptance letter, valid passport, proof of funds, health insurance, and the visa application form. After arriving in Spain, you must apply for your TIE (student residence card) within 30 days.
You should start the renewal process 60 days before your TIE expires (you can start up to 90 days before). Book an appointment at the Oficina de Extranjería or online via Sede Electrónica. You'll need: current TIE, passport, NIE, proof of enrollment or continued studies, proof of funds, and completed EX-17 form. Fees must be paid using the Modelo 790 form. After submitting, you'll receive a receipt (resguardo) that serves as proof while you wait.
The homologación process is handled by the Ministerio de Educación y Formación Profesional. You need to submit your original degree, official translation into Spanish by a sworn translator, legalization or Apostille of the document, and proof of identity. The process can take several months. GHASSA has compiled resources to guide you through this — check our Downloads section.
Yes! Students in Spain can work up to 30 hours per week without a separate work permit, as long as it doesn't interfere with your studies. Once you've completed your first year of studies in Spain, you can apply for an autorización de trabajo which allows you to work alongside your studies. Make sure to register with the Seguridad Social (Social Security) when you start any job.
Most Spanish banks require your NIE/TIE, passport, and proof of address. Some good options for international students include BBVA (has an English app), Santander, and digital banks like N26 or Wise which don't require a Spanish address. Wise in particular is very popular with international students as it has no monthly fees and great exchange rates.
International students with valid residence status can register with the public health system. Go to your local Centro de Salud (health centre) with your TIE, NIE, and proof of address (empadronamiento) to register and get your health card (tarjeta sanitaria). Many student visas also require private health insurance, which covers you from the start.
Empadronamiento is the official registration of your address in Spain at your local Ayuntamiento (town hall). It's required for many things including healthcare registration, certain administrative processes, and proof of residence. You need your passport/TIE and a rental contract or housing document to register. It's completely free.
Spain has a growing Ghanaian community, especially in cities like Madrid, Barcelona, Valencia, and Seville. GHASSA connects you with the community! You can also find African food stores in most major cities — areas near major train stations often have African markets. Join our WhatsApp group to get recommendations from community members in your city.
